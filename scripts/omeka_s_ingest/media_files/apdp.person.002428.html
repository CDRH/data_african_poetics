Biography

Ciríaco Bokesa Napo was born in 1939 in Equatorial Guinea. He studied in Salamanca, where he was ordained as a priest in 1969. He returned to Equatorial Guinea and was imprisoned during the Macías government for political reasons. Following his release, he worked as a teacher until 1992, when he was appointed deputy director of the Hispano-Guinean Cultural Center of Malabo. He became a professor at the Normal School of Malabo, at the Hispano-Guinean Cultural Center of Malabo, and at the School of Public Administration. He is the author of two collection of poems, Foam Voices and Chronicles of a Singular Journey.
