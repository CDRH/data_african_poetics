Biography

Chinua Achebe was born in Ogidi, Nigeria in 1930. He was a novelist, poet, and literary critic. He also worked for the Nigerian Broadcasting CorporationÂ from 1954 until just before the outbreak of the Nigeria-Biafra war. Regarded as one of the leading voices in African literature, Achebe has several publications including _Things Fall Apart _(1958), _No Longer at Ease_ (1960), and _Arrow of God_ (1964), among others. He won the Commonwealth Poetry Prize for his poetry debut, _Beware, Soul-Brother, and Other Poems _(1971). His other collections include _Christmas in Biafra and Other Poems_ (1973), _Another Africa_ (1998), and his _Collected Poems_ (2004). He has received numerous awards and honors celebrating his contributions to the literary arts. Achebe held a distinguished academic career lecturing at universities in Nigeria and the United States until he passed away in Boston, Massachusetts in 2013.
