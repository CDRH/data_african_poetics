Biography

Chinua Achebe was born in Ogidi, Nigeria, in 1930. Beyond being a prolific writer of prose and poetry, he also had a career in broadcasting. He went on to publish many books including novels, Things Fall Apart, No Longer at Ease, and Arrow of God. As a poet, he earned the Commonwealth Poetry Prize for his poetry debut, Beware, Soul-Brother, and Other Poems. He also published Christmas in Biafra and Other Poems, Another Africa, and Collected Poems. Later, he became a professor at Bard College and earned over 20 honorary degrees, recognizing his contributions to the literary arts.
