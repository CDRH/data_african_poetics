Biography

Josué Guébo was born 1972 in Abidjan, Côte d’Ivoire. A French poet, he is the author of several poetry collections, some of which have been translated into English. In 2014, he won the Tchicaya U Tam'si Prize for African Poetry for his book, Songe à Lampedusa (Think of Lampedusa). He is also the recipient of the 2017 Bernard Dadié Grand Prize. He explores themes of ethnic conflicts, political instability, and colonial and post-independence struggles. Guébo holds a PhD in History and Philosophy of Sciences. From 2011 to 2016, he served as president of the Ivorian Writers’ Association. He is a professor at the University of Félix Houphouët–Boigny of Cocody in Abidjan, Côte d’Ivoire
