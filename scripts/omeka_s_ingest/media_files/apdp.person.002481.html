Biography

Maher Sabry was born in 1967, in Cairo, Egypt. Mostly well known as a poet for his publications in various journals and anthologies, and for his collection Marionnette, he is also a playwright, graphic designer, and filmmaker. His poems explore sexuality and queer existence in Egyptian society. Sabry is also an LGBTQ activist, well known for his coverage of the notorious Queen Boat arrests in 2001 in Cairo, for which he was awarded the Felipa de Souza Award in 2002. He attended Cairo University and received a bachelor of arts degree in English, and studied cinema in San Francisco after seeking asylum in the U.S. He went on to a career in film and theater.
