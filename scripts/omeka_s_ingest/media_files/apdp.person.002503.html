Biography

Ribka Sibhatu was born in 1962 in Asmara, Eritrea. She is the author of the Tigrinya and Italian books of poetry, Aulò: Canto-Poesia Dall'Eritrea and Cittadino Che non Cè. L'immigrazione Nei Media Italiani. She was imprisoned in 1979 for refusing to marry an Ethiopian officer and released a year later. She fled the country, living in Ethiopia where she completed her secondary education and studied at the Istituto Tecnico Galileo Galilei. She moved to France afterwards, and then to Italy where she settled in Rome. Sibhatu received a doctor of philosophy degree in communication studies from La Sapienza. She worked for the city council in Rome from 2002 to 2005 as a consultant on inter-cultural politics. In 2006, she became a member of the scientific committee for inter-cultural affairs of the Italian Ministry of Education, Universities and Research. In 2012, she produced the documentary Aulò: Roma Postcoloniale.
