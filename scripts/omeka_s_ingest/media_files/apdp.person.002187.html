Biography

Bahia Mahmud Awah was born in 1960 in the disputed Western Sahara region. He is the author of several books, including The Teacher Who Taught Me on a Wooden Slate, Refugee Verses, Little Reflections and Poems, and How My Grandfather Almost Starved to Death. Due to the start of the Western Saharan war, he left home for Cuba where he obtained his undergraduate degree at the University of Havana studying telecommunications. He also received a Master's in Public Anthropology from the Autonomous University of Madrid, where he later became a professor of Social Anthropology.
