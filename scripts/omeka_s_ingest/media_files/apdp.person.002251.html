Biography

Finuala Dowling was born 1962 in Cape Town, South Africa. Her first poetry collection, I Flying (2002), was the recipient of the Ingrid Jonker Prize. Her other collections include winner of the Sanlam Award for Poetry, Doo-Wop Girls of the Universe, Penguin (2006), Notes from the Dementia Ward (2008) which won the Olive Schreiner Prize and Pretend You Don't Know Me (2008). She received an MA in English from the University of Cape Town (UCT) in 1986 and a D.Litt. from the University of South Africa (UNISA) in 1995. She was a Senior Lecturer at the Centre for Extra-Mural Studies, University of Cape Town from 2014-2021. Dowling is now a Freelance creative writing lecturer, and she also develops literary scripts which have been performed by John Maytham at a variety of venues in Western Cape.
