Biography

Assefa Gebre-Mariam Tessema was born in 1936 in Ethiopia. He is most well known for authoring Ethiopiaâ€™s national anthem, as well as for writing several books of poetry. He attended Addis Ababa University, after which he joined the Ministry of Education where he became associated with radical reformers. He taught in Ethiopia for several years before he was sent to the Soviet Union where he taught Amharic at Leningrad University. He worked for the Academy of Ethiopian Languages which published poetry, dictionaries, and other books in Ethiopian languages. He migrated to the United States at the beginning of the 21st century.
