Biography

Koumanthio Zeinab Diallo was born 1956 in Labé, Guinea and grew up in the Fulani culture of ancient Fouta Djallon. Novelist and playwright, she is wellknown for her advocacy for African women, her critiques against forced marriage and use of tales inscribed in oral literature to represent her country’s culture. Her poetry collection, Les rires du silence (The Joys of Silence) received the received a prize from the President of the Republuc of Guinea in 2005. In 1980, she earned an Engineering diploma from the Gamal Abdel Nasser University of Conakry. She set up Guinean branch of the PEN Club International and is a member of the International Committee of Women Writers. She is also the President of the International Commission of African Women Poets and Writers
