Biography

Youssef Rakha was born in 1976 in Cairo, Egypt. He obtained a bachelor's and master's degrees from Hull University, and went on to work as a journalist for Al-Ahram Weekly. He is the author of several books of poetry and has been selected as one of the best Arab writers under 40. He writes in English and Arabic and his work has been translated into Italian, French, German, Spanish, and Polish. His work is concerned with the role of obscenity in Arab society and the post-Muslim perspective. He co-founded the English language paper The National in Abu Dhabi in 2007.
