Biography

Makombo Bamboté was born in 1932 at Ouadda in the Haute-Kotto region. He is regarded as the first writer from Central African Republic to be published. His debut poetry collection is La poésie est dans l'histoire, which was published in 1960. He also authored the volume, Chant funèbre pour un héros d'Afrique, in 1962. Francophone poet, novelist and short story writer, he explored themes of identity, colonization, and the history of Central South Africans. He went to France in 1949 where he studied at École des hautes études en sciences sociales. Bamboté was appointed as an ambassador to UNESCO after General Jean-Bédel Bokassa's coup in 1974. He also served as a professor of African literature at the University of Laval. He died in 2016 in Quebec.
