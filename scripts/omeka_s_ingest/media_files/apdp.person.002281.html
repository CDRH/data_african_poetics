Biography

Aracelis Girmay was born 1977 in Santa Ana, CA to an American mother and Ethiopian father. Her first collection Teeth (2007) won the Great Lakes Colleges Association New Writers Award. Her second book, Kingdom Animalia, which examines illness, death, and shame, was a finalist for the National Book Critics Circle Award for Poetry in 2011. She went to Connecticut College for her undergrad in documentary studies and received her MFA from New York University. In 2011, Girmay was awarded a fellowship from the National Endowment for the Arts, and in 2015 she received a Whiting Award for Poetry. She is currently the Editor-at-Large for BOA Editions' Blessing the Boats Selections, and she also serves on the editorial board of the African Poetry Book Fund. She teaches at Hampshire College and lives in New York City.
