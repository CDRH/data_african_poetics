Biography

Samiya Bashir was born in 1970 in Ypsilanti, Michigan. A professor and lesbian poet, she is the author of poetry chapbooks Wearing Shorts on the First Day of Spring (1999), American Visa (2001) and Teasing Crow (2006). Her full-length collections include Where the Apple Falls (2005), Gospel (2009), a Lambda Literary Award finalist, and Field Theories (2017), which won the Oregon Book Award. As an activist, her work focuses on civil rights struggles and engages multiple landscapes of her own queer identity and politics. Bashir attended the University of California, Berkeley in 1994 where she received her BA in Literature of American Ethnic Cultures and served as Poet Laureate. In 2011, she studied Poetry for the People, an arts/activism program founded by June Jordan, in the University of Michigan. She was the first black woman to be awarded the Rome Prize by the American Academy in Rome in 2019. She joined the Reed College faculty in 2012, where she teaches poetry writing.
