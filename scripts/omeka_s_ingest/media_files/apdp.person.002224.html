Biography

Syl Cheney-Coker was born in 1945 in Freetown, Seirra Leone. A poet and a novelist, Cheney was awarded the 1991 Commonwealth Writers Prize (Africa Region) for his novel titled The Last Harmattan of Alusine Dunbar (1990). He is the author of critically acclaimed poetry collections such as Concerto for an Exile (1973), The Blood in the Desert’s Eyes (1990), The Graveyard Also Has Teeth (1974; revised edition, 1979), Stone Child and other poems (2008) and The Road to Jamica: Elegy for the Afro (2015). He attended the University of Oregon (1967-1970), University of California—Los Angeles (1970), and the University of Wisconsin—Madison (1971-72). He received the Fonlon-Nichols Award from the African Literature Association, and a fellowship from the International Writing program at the University of Iowa.
