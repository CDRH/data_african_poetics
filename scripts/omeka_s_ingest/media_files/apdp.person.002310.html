Biography

Jaji Tsitsi Ella was born in 1976 in Nyadire Mission, Zimbabwe. A poet and an associate professor of English at Duke University, she is the author of the award-winning academic book, Africa in Stereo: Modernism, Music, and Pan-African Solidarity, and poetry books such as Carnaval and Beating the Graves. She studied Piano and Literature at Oberlin College, and Comparative Literature at Cornell University. She has received fellowships from the National Endowment for the Humanities/Schomburg Center, Mellon Foundation, Radcliffe Institute for Advanced Studies, and National Humanities Center.
