Biography

Harry Garuba was born 1958 in Akure, Nigeria. At 24, he published his debut collection, Shadow and Dream and Other Poems (1982) which was first runner-up in the inaugural All-Africa Okigbo prize for poetry. In 1988, he received his MA and PhD from University of Ibadan, where he also served as an assistant lecturer in the English department from 1981 to 1997. Garuba was a member of the editorial advisory board of the Heinemann African Writers Series and was well known for his active interest in African and postcolonial literatures. In 1998, he moved to South Africa and joined the faculty at University of Cape Town in 2001. He died Cape Town at the age of 61.
