Biography

Patrice Nganang was born in 1970 in Yaoundé, Cameroon. Poet, novelist, and essayist, he is the author of poetry collections elobi (1995) and Apologie du Vandale (2006). As a literary scholar, his work investigates subjects related to violence, postcolonial African literature and cultures. In 1992, he received his MA from the University of Yaoundé. He also earned his Ph.D. in comparative literature at the Johann Wolfgang Goethe University. In 2003, he was Shortlisted for the Fonlon-Nichols Award for Extraordinary Achivement in Scholarship and literature of the African Literature Association (ALA). Nganang is a Full Professor of Literary and Cultural Theory at State University of New York, Stony Brook.
