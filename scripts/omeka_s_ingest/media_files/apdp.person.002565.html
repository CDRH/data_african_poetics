Biography

Abdourahman Waberi was born in 1965 in Djibouti. He is most well known for his books The land without Shadows, Transit, In the United States of Africa and Passage des Larmes. He studied English literature in France and went on to obtain a doctoral degree on emigrant writers at the Paris-Sorbonne. He worked as a literary consultant for Editions Le Serpent Ã  Plumes, Paris, and as a literary critic for Le Monde Diplomatique. He continued on to become a professor of French, francophone studies and creative writing at George Washington University.
