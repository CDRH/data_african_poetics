Biography

John Eppel was born 1947 in Lydenburg, South Africa and grew up at Colleen Bawn, Zimbabwe. He is the author of several books of poetry including Spoils of War (1989), which won the Ingrid Jonker prize, Songs My Sonata for Matabeleland (1995), Country Taught Me (2005). Poet and novelist, he is known to write about the indigenous white experience in Zimbabwe and incorporate satire in his work to criticize political and financial corruption in Zimbabwe. His goal with prose is “to ridicule greed, cruelty, self-righteousness and related vices like racism, sexism, jingoism, and homophobia.” He received an MA English from the University of Natal in South Africa and now teaches English at Christian Brothers College in Bulawayo.
