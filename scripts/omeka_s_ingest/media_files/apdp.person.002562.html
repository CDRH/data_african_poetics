Biography

Mongane Wally Serote was born in 1944 in South Africa. He became involved in the Black Consciousness Movement while in high school. He was linked to the group "Soweto Poets,‚Äù and his poems took on themes of political activism, the development of black identity, revolt, and resistance. In 1969, he was arrested by the apartheid government under the Terrorism Act and spent nine months in solitary confinement, before being released without charge. He attended Columbia University, from which he received a master's degree. He lived in exile in Gaborone, Botswana for several years but later returned to South Africa. He is the author of several collections of poems.
