Biography

Marcel Kemadjou Njanke was born in 1970 in Douala, Cameroon. In 1994, he won the Young Central African Poetry award after which he published his first collection, Cries of the Soul. He went on to publish Poto-poto Blues, another collection of poems, and also a novel. He lives in Douala where he runs Open Book, an association that promotes reading. He started the movement “Racontages” which unites the oral literatures and written literatures that highlight African culture. Racontages has been studied in schools, and it has inspired a 2014 symposium at the University of Buea.
