Biography

Abdelhamid Laghouati was born in 1943 in Berrouaghia, Algeria. He is the author of several books of poetry, including: Poèmes indigènes, Comme Toujours, L'Oued noir, and De Si Belles Insultes. He had a career as a teacher for many years before meeting famous Algerian poet Jean Sénac who was instrumental in his poetry career. He became one of the members of Awechem, a movement created by a group of influential artists after the independence of Algeria which advocated for and defended popular art. In 2000, he moved to France where he eventually died in 2021.
