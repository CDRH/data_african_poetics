Biography

Donald Parenzee was born in 1948 in South Africa. He attended the University of Cape Town where he obtained a bachelor's degree in architecture. He was the author of two poetry collections, Driven to Work and No Free Sleeping. He worked in various capacities, practicing and teaching architecture and working as an exhibitions curator at the District Six Museum. He also ran creative workshops, exploring ways to integrate literature and visual art. He participated in many initiatives opposing apartheid and racism over the course of his lifetime. He died in 2022 in South Africa.
