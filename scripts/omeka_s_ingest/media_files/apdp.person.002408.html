Biography

Amel Moussa was born in 1971 in Tunisia. She is the author of two books of poetry: Ontha al-ma (The water female) and Khajal al-yakout (The Emerald's Bashfulness). She obtained a Master's in sociology from the University of Tunis and went on to work as a journalist, consultant, and professor of Press and Communication Sciences for many years. She is the recipient of Tunisia's National Creative Award for poetry, and the 2006 Arab Woman Organization's Award for journalism. Her poetry has been translated into several languages such as Italian, English, Spanish, French, Polish, and Czech. In 2021, she was appointed the Minister of Family, Women, Children, and the Elderly.
