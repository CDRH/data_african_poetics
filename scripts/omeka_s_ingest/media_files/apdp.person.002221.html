Biography

Bole Butake was born in 1947 in Nkor Noni, North West Province of Cameroon. Poet and playwright, he is well known for the agitprop nature of his work and writing on issues of corruption and governance, as well as his contribution to the arts and culture in Cameroon. He received his BA in Modern English Letters from the University of Yaounde and his MA in English Literature from the University of Leeds. He also earned his PhD in 1983 from the Faculty of Letters and Social Sciences, University of Yaoundé. Butake continued on to become a professor of Performing Arts and African Literature at University of Yaounde until he retired in 2012. He died in 2016 in Yaounde, Central Province, Cameroon.
