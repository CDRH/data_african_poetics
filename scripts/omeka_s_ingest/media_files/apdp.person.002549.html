Biography

Ahmad Yamani was born in 1970 in Cairo, Egypt. He graduated from Cairo University in 1992, receiving a bachelor of arts degree in Arabic literature. He also received a doctorate in Arabic philology from Complutense University in Madrid. He went on to work at the broadcaster RTVE. He has published several books of poetry in Arabic, and one in his adopted language Spanish. Yamani has translated numerous Spanish-language writers into Arabic, among them José Ángel Valente, Rubén Darío, César Vallejo, Adolfo Bioy Casares, Miguel Casado, Rosendo Tello, Ángel Guinda, Agustín Porras, and Roberto Bolaño. His collections of poems include: Mid Rooms, Wrong Places, and Flowers in the Head.
