Biography

Liyou Libsekal was born in 1990 in Addis Ababa, Ethiopia. Poet and essayist, she is the author of chapbook, Bearing Heavy Things (2015), part of the African Poetry Book Fund’s New Generation African Poets series. Her poems explore themes of displacement, culture, home, and identity. She also writes about the influences of modernization and globalization on Ethiopia’s economic growth and transformation. In 2014, Libsekal won the Brunel University African Poetry Prize for her poem “Riding Chinese Machines.” She earned her BA in Anthropology from George Washington University. She lives in Addis Ababa, Ethiopia.
